Common Reasons for Cranking But No Start

If your car cranks but doesn't start, several factors could be at play. Here are some common issues:

1. Ignition System Problems: Issues with spark plugs, ignition coils, or the distributor can prevent proper ignition.

2. Fuel Delivery Issues: A faulty fuel pump, clogged fuel filter, or injectors can lead to inadequate fuel supply.

3. Engine Sensors Malfunction: Faulty sensors like the crankshaft position sensor can disrupt engine timing.

4. Compression Problems: Low compression due to worn piston rings or valves can hinder combustion.

5. Timing Belt or Chain Failure: A broken or slipped timing belt/chain can lead to misalignment of engine components.
